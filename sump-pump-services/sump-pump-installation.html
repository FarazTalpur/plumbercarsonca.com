Frequently Asked Questions

A sump pump is installed to prevent basement flooding and to manage groundwater around your home's foundation. It helps to keep your basement dry by pumping out water that accumulates in a sump pit, typically located in the basement or crawlspace.

The size of the sump pump you need depends on several factors, including the size of your basement, the volume of groundwater, and the distance the water needs to be pumped. Generally, a 1/3 horsepower pump is sufficient for most average-sized basements, but larger areas or high water flow situations may require a 1/2 horsepower pump or larger.

A sump pump should run as often as needed, depending on the amount of water in the sump pit. During heavy rainstorms or spring snowmelts, it may run frequently or even continuously. It's important to regularly check the pump's functionality and ensure it cycles on and off correctly.

While it is possible to install a sump pump by yourself if you have the necessary skills and tools, it is often recommended to hire a professional. Proper installation is crucial for ensuring optimal performance, avoiding potential water damage, and meeting local building codes.

A typical sump pump lasts between 7 to 10 years. Regular maintenance, such as cleaning the pump and checking the float switch, can help prolong its lifespan. It's also advisable to test the pump periodically to ensure it functions correctly when needed.
